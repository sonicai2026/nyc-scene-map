🍽️ Restaurants (Seats 10+ at 7pm)

1. Smithy's Tavern

Grill & PubGroups
THE Alpe d'Huez institution. 25+ years running. Burgers, Tex-Mex, grilled meats, fajitas. Massive space — handles big groups easily. Covers band starts 6pm, table dancing by 10pm. Book for 6:30, claim your table, never leave. Flows straight into L'Igloo nightclub downstairs.
📍 Old Town (Vieil Alpe) 💶 €€ 📞 +33 4 76 80 34 44

2. Au Montagnard

French Alpine
Classic French bistro. Fondue, raclette, tartiflette, pierrade, grilled meats. Cozy mountain interior. Seats 40 inside + 30 on terrace. Extensive menu — something for everyone. Perfect for a proper French mountain dinner with the crew.
📍 Town center 💶 €€ 📞 +33 4 76 11 33 34

3. La Ferme d'Hubert

Upscale French
Gastronomic Alpine dining. Local produce, traditional methods, refined presentation. Attached to the 5-star Hotel Daria-I Nor. Ski-in/ski-out for lunch, gorgeous for dinner. Your "nice dinner" option. Sunny terrace + chic interior.
📍 Near Telecabin Alpes Express 💶 €€€ 📞 +33 4 76 11 21 21

4. Le Roy Ladre

Italian
Best pizza in Alpe d'Huez. Fresh dough, incredible toppings. Also pasta and steaks. Tucked away on cobbled street in old town. Several large tables — good for 10. Unassuming but beloved. Takeaway available too. Get there early or book ahead.
📍 Old Town 💶 €€ 📞 +33 4 76 11 32 15

5. La Cabane

Mountain ExperienceOn-Mountain
THE bucket-list dinner. They drag you up the mountain in a piste basher (grooming machine), you eat fondue under the stars at altitude, then ride back down. Costs a fortune but unforgettable with a group. Book well in advance — this sells out.
📍 On-mountain (transport included) 💶 €€€€ 📞 +33 6 79 55 09 97

6. La Plage des Neiges

French BrasserieSlopeside
Open lunch and dinner. Slopeside location with huge terrace — great sunset views. Cozy interior for evening. Good for groups who want a relaxed dinner without trekking into town. Brasserie-style menu.
📍 Slopeside 💶 €€

7. Au Puits des Saveurs

French-Asian Fusion
Chef Patrice Blot's fusion cuisine — French classics with exotic Asian touches. More refined and sophisticated. Good change of pace from cheese-heavy Alpine food after a few days. Seats groups comfortably.
📍 Town center 💶 €€€

🍺 Après-Ski (On-Mountain)

La Folie Douce ⭐ MUST-DO

Après-Ski2,300m
The main event. Top of Marmottes lift at 2,300m. Opens for breakfast, but the madness starts at 2:30pm. Professional dancers, DJs from Ibiza, 3,000 people dancing in ski boots. Champagne spraying. Table dancing. Absolute carnival energy. Think Tomorrowland on a mountain. Plan to be destroyed after.
⏰ Après from 2:30pm daily 💶 Beer ~€10, Cocktails ~€15 💡 Arrive by 2pm or book VIP table

La Folie Douce Fruitière Restaurant

Après + Dining2,300m
The refined side of Folie Douce — smart mountain restaurant with panoramic views, sun-drenched terrace. Great for a proper slopeside lunch before the après madness kicks off next door.
📍 Top of Marmottes lift 💶 €€€

Sporting Bar

Après-Ski
By the ice rink, ski-accessible. Leather chairs, 70s vibes, used to be the French ski team's hangout. Owner is connected — occasional celebrity sightings. More sophisticated après option if Folie Douce is too much.
📍 Near ice rink 💶 €€

FreeRide Bar

Après-Ski
At the foot of the slopes. Founded by three ski instructor friends. Authentic, warm, full of mountain personality. Great first-beer-off-the-mountain spot. Less chaotic than Folie Douce.
📍 Base of slopes 💶 €

Nightclub
THE nightclub of Alpe d'Huez. Opens at midnight when Smithy's kicks everyone out. Foam parties, R&B nights, big-name European DJs. Electro-heavy but varies. This is where the night ends (or begins). Below Smithy's — the natural flow is dinner → Smithy's → L'Igloo.
⏰ Midnight – late 📍 Below Smithy's, Old Town 💶 €€

💡 Pro Tips for 10 People

📞 BOOKING: Call restaurants directly. Most don't use online platforms. Say "reservation for 10 at 19h" in English — they all speak it. Book 1-2 weeks ahead for March.
🧀 COOK NIGHTS: Hit the local Sherpa supermarket for raclette cheese, charcuterie, wine, and baguettes. A raclette night at the Airbnb with 10 people and cheap wine is one of the best meals you'll have.
🎿 THE FLOW: Ski → La Folie Douce (2:30pm) → ski down half-drunk → FreeRide Bar at base → Airbnb to shower → dinner at 7pm → Smithy's → L'Igloo. This is the Alpe d'Huez playbook.
🍷 WINE: French wine is cheap in resort. Order carafes of house red at restaurants — it's usually great and half the price of bottles. Stock up at Sherpa for Airbnb nights.
💶 BUDGET: Mountain restaurants and Folie Douce drinks are expensive (€10+ beers). Town restaurants are reasonable. Smithy's is mid-range. Cook nights save real money for a group of 10.
🗓️ THURSDAY: This is THE night in Alpe d'Huez. Brits go hardest on Thursdays. Plan your best dinner + après for Thursday night. Smithy's + L'Igloo will be peak energy.
